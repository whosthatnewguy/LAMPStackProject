Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Order of play - measures 1-9, 2-7, 10-18, 11-18, 1-6, 19-21
"Wer will, kann das ganze Stück einmal wiederholen, bevor er D.C.
al + - + spielt." - Maria Linnemann
(If you want, you can repeat the whole thing once before following the
instruction D.C. al + - +.)

left hand fingering below tab       right hand fingering above tab
1 - index finger                    p - thumb
2 - middle finger                   i - index finger
3 - ring finger                     m - middle finger
4 - little finger                   a - ring finger

===    - note held, length of note, duration
=-=    - let the note ring (don't dampen the string)
>      - (above the tab) accented, stressed
t      - (above the tab) tenuto - slightly accented, played firmly -
         the note is held for at least its full length or a little longer
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
+      - (end of measure 6) Coda sign (jump to the Coda)
bpm    - beats per minute
cresc. - becoming gradually louder
D.C.   - Da Capo - return to the start (al +  - and play till +)
F.     - Fermata - pause, note(s) held
g      - grace note (ornament) - extra note(s) played immediately before (or
         at the same time as) the main note, then immediately released
mf     - mezzoforte - moderately loud
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft
pp     - pianissimo - very soft
sempre - always (sempre cresc. - continuing to get louder)

octave harmonics -
<12>   - natural (open) - on the 12th fret
2<14>  - artificial - left hand 2nd fret, right hand points to the 14th fret
4<16>  - artificial - left hand 4th fret, right hand points to the 16th fret
