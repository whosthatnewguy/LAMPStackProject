Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
This piece was written in August-September 1966. It was first published in 2018.
The MIDI is 48 bpm (dotted quarter notes).

Olivier Chasson makes editorial changes in the sheet from the unpublished
manuscript in the following measures: 4, 9, 16, 23, 29, 30, 31

measures 1,2 - these measures have been changed from the sheet to avoid the
               discords caused by the G (open 3rd string) -

left hand fingering below tab       right hand fingering above tab
1 - index finger                    p - thumb
2 - middle finger                   i - index finger
3 - ring finger                     m - middle finger
4 - little finger (pinky)           a - ring finger

===    - notes held, length of notes
=-=    - let the note ring, don't dampen the sound
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
< < <  - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
> > >  - decrescendo - becoming gradually softer
bpm    - beats per minute
c2     - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret
C4     - barre on the 4th fret
cresc. - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
dim.   - diminuendo - diminishing, gradually getting softer
f      - forte
F.     - Fermata - pause, note(s) held
g      - grace note - ornament - an extra note played just before the main
         note and immediately released - the following note is usually
         played as a hammer-on, a pull-off or sometimes a slide
h      - hammer-on
meno   - less (meno f - less loudly)
mf     - mezzoforte - moderately loud
mp     - mezzopiano - moderately soft
msfz   - mezzo-sforzando - semi-accented, moderately accented
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
poco   - little (poco f - a little louder) (poco rit. - a little slower)
rit.   - ritenuto - slowing down, suddenly slower
subito - suddenly (f subito - suddenly loud)
t      - (above the tab) tenuto - stressed, slightly accented - played firmly -
         the note is held for at least its full length
tempo  - speed (a tempo - return to the earlier tempo)
