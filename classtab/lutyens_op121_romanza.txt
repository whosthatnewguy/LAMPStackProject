Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
[My main purpose in making this tab was to see if it could be done! - Weed]

Elisabeth Lutyen's contribution to the music scores of 1960's UK horror films -
https://moviesandmania.com/2014/04/21/elisabeth-lutyens-composer/

The sheet is a facsimile of the composer's hand-written score. Sometimes the
composer's intentions are not obvious [suggestions welcome]. The tab lines are
numbered so that they refer to the lines of the original manuscript. The sheet
isn't clear how the harmonics should be played - they have been tabbed as octave
harmonics for convenience. The sheet rarely gives help for left hand fingering.

The suggested total length of the piece is between 8 min 30 sec and 9 minutes.

===     - note held, length of note, duration
=-=     - let the notes continue to ring, don't dampen the sound
|-3-|   - triplet - 3 notes in the time of 2 notes
< < <   - (below the tab) crescendo - becoming gradually louder
> > >   - (below the tab) decrescendo - becoming gradually softer
> > >o  - (below the tab - decrescendo - fading away to silence?
/       - slide the note up (glissando)
'       - breath mark - slight pause
|-3-|   - triplet - 3 notes in the time of 2 notes
/-3"-\  - ? maybe 3 seconds of silence?
arp.    - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
bpm     - beats per minute (quarter notes), | = 1 quarter note
f       - forte - loud
ff      - fortissimo - very loud
fff     - fortississimo - extremely loud
F.      - Fermata - note(s) held, or longer pause (rest)
g       - grace note - an extra note played immediately before the main note
nf      - mezzoforte - moderately loud
mp      - mezzopiano - moderately soft
p       - piano - soft
pizz.   - Bartok pizzicato (the strings are snapped against the fingerboard)
poco    - little, a little (poco f - a little louder)
pont.   - ponte - the bridge (sul pont. - play near the bridge of the guitar)
pp      - pianissimo - very soft
s       - staccato - detached, cut off, the note is not held for its full value
sfz     - sforzando - strongly accented, suddenly loud
sffz    - sforzatissimo - very strongly accented, suddenly very loud
tasto   - fingerboard (sul tasto - play near or above the fingerboard)
tempo   - speed (a tempo - return to the earlier/original speed)
x       - tambora - hit the strings close to the bridge with side of the hand

<12>    - natural (open) (octave) harmonic on the 12th fret
1<13>   - art. harmonic, left hand 1st fret, right hand points to the 13th fret
2<14>   - art. harmonic, left hand 2nd fret, right hand points to the 14th fret
etc
