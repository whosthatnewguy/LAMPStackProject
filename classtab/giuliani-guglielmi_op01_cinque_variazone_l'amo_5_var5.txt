Notes and Legend
----------------
"I Capuleti e i Montecchi (The Capulets and the Montagues) is an Italian
opera (Tragedia lirica) in two acts by Vincenzo Bellini. The libretto by
Felice Romani was a reworking of the story of Romeo and Juliet for an opera
by Nicola Vaccai called Giulietta e Romeo and is based on the play of the
same name by Luigi Scevola written in 1818, thus an Italian source rather
than taken directly from William Shakespeare." (Wikipedia)
Emilia Giuliani-Guglielmi's Op 1 was first published by Gio. Ricordi in 1834.
The sheet title is Variation 5, but it's clearly the 'Finale'.
The DGA edition doesn't give details of left hand fingering so there are
probably better choices as to which strings to use. (Comment are welcome!)
The tempo of the MIDI is 96 bpm (quarter notes).
Order of play: 0-8, 0-8, (8)-42.

measure 9 - in the sheet the 3rd notes of the 3rd & 4th beats are A# (not A)
measure 10 - in the sheet the 2nd note of the 3rd beat is Bb (not B)
measure 11 - in the sheet the 2nd note of the 4th beat is C# (not C)
measures 20,21,26,27,38,39 - in the sheet the triplets are not marked

left hand fingering below the tab -
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

===   - note held, length of note, duration
|-3-| - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
F.    - (long) pause (after playing the theme and all 5 variations!)
h     - hammer-on
p     - (below the tab) piano - soft
p     - (between the notes) pull-off
sf    - sforzando - forced, strongly accented, suddenly louder

c5    - part barre on the 5th fret
c7    - part barre on the 7th fret
c9    - part barre on the 9th fret
