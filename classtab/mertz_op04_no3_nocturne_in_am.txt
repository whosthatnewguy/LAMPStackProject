Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The starting MIDI tempo for the Maestoso is 72 bpm (quarter notes).
The starting tempo for the Vivace is 116 bpm (quarter notes).

measure 11 - in the sheet the 2nd note of the 2nd beat is G (not G#), and the
             1st note of the 3rd beat and the 2nd note of the 4th beat are both
             F# (not F) - some guitarists play the F#s and perhaps this is
             correct, but it creates a major scale which seems strange to me
             - here is the original sheet (except with the G corrected to G#) -

                                  *     *           *
                  11  |--3--|  |--3--|  |--3--|  |--3--|
                  e|-----------7--4--0-h2-----------------|
                  B|-----------------------3--0-----------|
                  G|-----------------------------1--------|
                  D|7g6--5--6-----------------------4-p2--|
                  A|--------------------------------------|
                  E|--0===================================|

measures 12,13 - the 6th & 7th notes are a dotted 16th note and a 32nd note
measure 34 - if the 3rd note (Ab) sounds 'quirky', play A (2nd fret) instead
measure 40 - only the bottom note of the chord is accented (A, 5th string)

===     - note held
>       - (above the tab) accented, marked, stressed
|-3-|   - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
< < <   - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
> > >   - decrescendo - becoming gradually softer
arp.    - arpeggiate - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
bpm     - beats per minute
cresc.  - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
f       - forte - loud
ff      - fortissimo - very loud
F.      - Fermata - note(s) held, pause
g       - (measure 11) grace note - ornament - an extra note played just before
          the main note - the next note is played as a pull-off
h       - hammer-on
p       - (between the notes) pull-off
p       - (below the tab) piano - soft
ritard. - ritardando - slowing down, gradually slower
s       - staccato - detached, cut off, the note is not held for its full value
sf      - sforzando - forced, strongly accented, suddenly louder
