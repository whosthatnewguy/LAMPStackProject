Notes and Legend
----------------
The ending is an example of a Picardy cadence, common in Volume I of Bach's
Well Tempered Klavier, where a piece in a minor key ends with a major 3rd
or a chord containing it.
A simple arrangement of this piece is to play all the upper notes together
with the lower notes which fall on the 1st note of each beat.
The Pavel Shatsky video uses the Alfredo Sanchez transcription.
The Michael Ladehoff video is based on an arrangement by Alexander Vynograd.
Use a capo on the 3rd fret to play the Prelude in its original key.
The tempo of the MIDI is 76 bpm (quarter notes) - some people play it faster!
