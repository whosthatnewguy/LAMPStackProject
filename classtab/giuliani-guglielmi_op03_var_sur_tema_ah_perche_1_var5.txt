Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
"La sonnambula (The Sleepwalker) is an opera semiseria in two acts, with music
in the bel canto tradition by Vincenzo Bellini set to an Italian libretto by
Felice Romani, based on a scenario for a ballet-pantomime written by Eugene
Scribe and choreographed by Jean-Pierre Aumer called La somnambule, ou L'arrivee
d'un nouveau seigneur. ...The first performance took place at the Teatro Carcano
in Milan on 6 March 1831." (Wikipedia)

Emilia Giuliani-Guglielmi's Op 3 was first published by Gio. Ricordi in 1836.

The DGA edition gives little information about the left hand fingering, so feel
free to experiment where the fingering isn't given. There are many leaps up and
down the fretboard in the tab, but similar leaps are found in the sheet music of
other compositions by Emilia Giuliani-Guglielmi, which as a virtuoso performer
she probably enjoyed playing in her concerts. The tempo of the MIDI is 76 bpm
(beats per minute - quarter notes) - perhaps it should be faster? or slower?

left hand fingering below the tab -
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

===    - note held, length of note, duration
/      - slide the note up
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes - though here they are
         formed of a quarter note and an eighth note (= 3 eighth notes)
C3     - barre on the 3rd fret
c8     - part barre on the 8th fret
h      - hammer-on
F.     - Fermata - note(s) held, pause
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
sf     - sforzando - suddenly louder, stressed
~      - turn - ornament - play the scale note above, the main note, the note
         below (usually a semitone below), and finally the main note again
