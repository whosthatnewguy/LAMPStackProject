Notes and Legend
----------------
Editions used: Robert Brojer (Schott 44486, Mainz, 1979) (Vienna MS)
               Ruggero Chiesa (Edizioni Suvini Zerboni, Milan) (London MS)
               Jean-Daniel Forget, revised Guy Grangereau (Dresden MS)
               Jean-Daniel Forget (Vienna MS)

Some of the notes on open strings and lower frets can be played on closed
strings and higher frets to achiece better phrasing.
Only some of the ornaments in the original MSS are shown - feel free to add
your own. Similarly some of the bass notes can be moved up or down an octave,
or can be doubled (either the same note played on 2 strings or played together
with a note one octave higher or lower).

The tempo of the Meredith Connie video and the MIDI is 72 bpm (quarter notes).

measure 7 - in the Dresden and London editions the C# on the 4th beat is missing
measure 8 - the Chiesa and Forget editions have both G# and A on the 1st beat
            but Brojer moves the A to the 2nd note (which is more playable)
measure 15 - the Chiesa edition has a B (5th string 2nd fret) (quarter note)
             on the 4th beat (together with the F#)
measure 16 - the Brojer edition has a B (5th string 2nd fret) in the chord
measure 19 - the London and Dresden editions have D (2nd string 3rd fret) on
             the 4th beat (not A)
measure 22 - the Brojer edition has G# (6th string) on the 1st beat (not F#)
measure 22 - in the Vienna edition the chord on the 3rd beat also has a D#
             (2nd string 3rd fret)
measure 23 - in the Brojer edition the 5th note is A (1st string, 5th fret)
             (not E#, 2nd string 6th fret) - the A is easier on the ear!
measure 24 - the Chiesa edition has C# (not B#) in the chord on the 1st beat
measure 30 - the C# on the 1st beat is only in the Forget edition
measure 32 - the Forget/Grangereau edition has the following -

===  - length of note, note held, duration
arp. - arpeggiate - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
g    - grace note (ornament) - extra note(s) played at the same time as
       (or just before) the main note, then immediately released - the
       following note is usually played as a hammer-on, pull-off, or slide
h    - hammer-on
p    - (between the notes) pull-off
W    - inverted mordent (ornament) - play the main note, quickly followed by
       the note below, then quickly back to the original note
c2   - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2   - barre on the 2nd fret
C4   - barre on the 4th fret
c5   - part barre on the 5th fret
c7   - part barre on the 7th fret
C7   - barre on the 7th fret
