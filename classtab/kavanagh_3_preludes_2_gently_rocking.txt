Notes and Legend
----------------
"Allow as many strings to ring as possible.
Keep the bass always in the background."

The two Dale Kavanagh recordings are ~80 bpm (quarter notes) which is slower
than the tempo given in the sheet. The Conni Sheu video is much slower.
The tempo of the MIDI is 76 bpm (quarter notes).

measure 30 - in the sheet the E on the 1st beat is not tied through to the
             end of the measure, but is replayed with the C#

left hand fingering below tab       right hand fingering above tab
1 - index finger                    p - thumb
2 - middle finger                   i - index finger
3 - ring finger                     m - middle finger
4 - little finger                   a - ring finger

===    - length of note, note held, duration
,      - (end of measure 14) slight pause, breath mark
< < <  - crescendo - getting louder
> > >  - decrescendo - getting softer
F.     - (end of measure 22) Fermata - pause
f      - forte - loud
h      - hammer-on
mf     - mezzoforte - moderately loud
mp     - mezzopiano - moderately soft
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft
pp     - pianissimo - very soft
ppp    - pianississimo - extremely soft
rit.   - slowing down, suddenly slower

metalico - metallic sound - play near the bridge (sul ponticello)
nat. - play naturally (?)
porto. - ?
pulpe - play with the flesh of the thumb (not the nail)
