"The starry sky, summoned forth here by the clear timbres of harmonics,
illuminates the enormous, baroque cathedral in Santiago de Compostela, a city
that exists simply because of faith in a miracle. Legend says that St James's
body, after having been beheaded by Herod, was wondrously transported in seven
days by the Saint's disciples to northern Spain, where it lay forgotten for
hundreds of years. In 813, starlight guided a shepherd to a site, on which he
discovered the Saint's remains. Over this spot stands the cathedral erected
during the 11th, 12th and 13th centuries, in which the relics of St James are
entombed, and to which hundreds of millions of pilgrims have journeyed from the
middle ages until today." - Michael Lorimer (editor)

Notes and Legend
----------------
"The performer of Postcards from Spain will find it impossible to sustain every
note for its written value. Neverthless, it is always possible to communicate
the composer's intention, and therefore the score is presented in this edition
as Thea Musgrave wrote it, and as she hopes the listener will imagine it, rather
than as a precise depiction of the literal sound that one performer might
produce. The ears of the listener, rather then the fingers of the performer,
guide the fingering. Whilst this fingering is a model that pleases the composer
and the editor, the score is presented as the composer conceived it, and other
fingerings are possible."
 - Michael Lorimer (editor)

Suggested duration: 1 min 45 sec
