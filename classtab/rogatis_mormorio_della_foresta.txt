Notes & Legend
--------------
The fingering is taken from the sheet.
The tempo of the MIDI is ~76 bpm (quarter notes), but it needs a much better
interpretation to give the mood of the piece (as in the Paolo Lambiase video).

measures 55-66 - these are the same as measures 1-12

left hand fingering below the tab -
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

===   - notes held, length of note, duration
>     - accented, stressed
arp.  - rip[ple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
F.    - pause, note(s) held
g     - grace note (ornament) - in the sheet these are shown as appoggiaturas
        (passing notes), extra note(s) leading toward the main note, usually
        played as hammer-ons, pull-offs, or slides
p     - pull-off

CI    - barre on the 1st fret
cII   - part barre on the 2nd fret
cIII  - part barre on the 3rd fret
CIII  - barre on the 3rd fret

< < <    - (below the tab) crescendo - growing louder
> > >    - (below the tab) decrescendo - growing softer
a tempo  - return to the original tempo
cantando - in a singing style, cantabile, graceful and well blended
cresc.   - crescendo - growing louder
dim.     - diminuendo - diminishing, gradually getting softer
dolce    - sweetly
doloroso - sad, sorrowful
e        - and (rall. e - rallentando and)
f        - forte - loud
ma       - but (ma piano - but soft)
mf       - mezzoforte - moderately loud
molto    - very, much (rall. molto - slowing right down)
mp       - mezzopiano - moderately soft
p        - piano - soft
pp       - pianissimo - very soft
rall.    - rallentando - slowing down (often becoming gradually softer)
