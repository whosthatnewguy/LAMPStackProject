Notes and Legend
----------------
The sheet gives the tempo as 168 bpm (dotted quarter notes), but based on
the videos the tempo should be half that (84 bpm, dotted quarter notes).
The tempo of the demo MIDI is ~76 bpm (dotted quarter notes).

The sheet has a Segno sign ($) at the start of measure 9, and a Coda symbol (+)
together with the text "Dal $ al +" at the end of measure 21. However none of
the three reference videos follow this instruction exactly. The Massimo Delle
Cese video repeats from the beginning (not from measure 9), and the Antonio
Pezzullo video ignores the Dal Segno instruction completely, playing -
     measures 1-8, 1-8, 9-12, 9-11, 13-32
My interpretation of the instruction is -
     measures 1-8, 1-8, 9-12, 9-11, 13-21, 9-11, 13-32
(A 2nd Coda symbol, at the start of measure 22, would make it clearer, as the
1st Coda symbol in a piece is usually an instruction to jump to the start of
the Coda, the end section, which is indicated with a 2nd Coda symbol.)

measure 9 - in the sheet the 1st A (6th string) is a half note (not dotted)
measure 11 - in the sheet the 1st F (6th string) is a half note (not dotted)

left hand fingering below tab       right hand fingering above tab
1 - index finger                    p - thumb
2 - middle finger                   i - index finger
3 - ring finger                     m - middle finger
4 - little finger                   a - ring finger

===    - length of note, note held, duration
$      - (measure 9) Segno - sign (Dal Segno - go back and play from the sign)
+      - Coda symbol (al + - play until the Coda symbol)
F.     - Fermata - pause (at the end, before starting the next study)
C1     - barre on the 1st fret with the 1st finger
C5     - barre on the 5th fret with the 1st finger
