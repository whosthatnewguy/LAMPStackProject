Notes and Legend
----------------
This is a difficult three part fugue. I've tried to show the different parts
in the left hand fingering below the tab, so it will sometimes look strange!
The ending is an example of a Picardy cadence, common in Vol I of Bach's Well
Tempered Klavier, where a piece in a minor key ends with the major chord.
Use a capo on the 3rd fret to play the Prelude in its original key.
The tempo of the MIDI is 66 bpm (quarter notes) (maybe too fast for a guitar?)
The fingering in the sheet is unclear in places, with longer notes sometimes
cut off by another note played on the same string.

measure 13 - 0* - the 1st part goes down to D (open 4th string) while the
             the 3rd part goes down to B (open 2nd string)
measure 24 - in the sheet the D on the 2nd beat (2nd string, 3rd fret) is a
             half note tied to an eighth note in the following measure
measure 25 - 3* - the same note, C (3rd note of the 4th beat, 4th string
             10th fret) is used in both the 1st and 3rd parts of the Fugue
measure 26 - the sheet provides an ossia (alternative way of playing) -
