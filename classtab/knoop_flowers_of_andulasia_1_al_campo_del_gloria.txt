The term 'drum' presumably means played in the style of a drum, perhaps by
tapping on the body of the guitar with the fist? Similarly 'cornet' probably
means that the melody is to be played in the style of a cornet.

measure 18 - in the sheet the 3rd note is G# (not A) (compare measure 66)
measure 20 - in the sheet there is also a C# (3rd string 6th fret) on the
             1st beat (compare measure 68)
measures 26,27 - on the 4th beat, only the lower note is played staccato
measure 32 - on the 1st beat, only the 2 lower notes are played staccato
measures 33,37,39 - the 16th notes in the tab are 32nd notes in the sheet
measure 60 - in the sheet the 4th note is D (not D#) (compare measure 12)

left hand fingering below the tab -
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

===   - notes held, length of note, duration
/     - slide up
\     - slide down
|-3-| - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
arp.  - arpeggiate - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
ff    - fortissimo - very loud
g     - grace note - acciaccatura - an extra note played at the same time as
        or just before the main note, played as a hammer-on, pull-off or slide
glis. - glissando - slide the note allowing the intervening notes to be heard
h     - hammer-on
s     - staccato - detached, cut off, the note is not held for its full value
c2    - part barre on the 2nd fret
C4    - barre on the 4th fret
