Notes and Legend
----------------
editions: Eva Jaksch - Werner Icking Music Archive, 2002
          Edmund Wensiecki - Friedrich Hofmeister FH 4035, Leipzig, 1965
          Editio Musica Budapest Z8309 (Hungary)
          Eythor Thorlaksson - The Guitar School 1125, Iceland
          Jean Francois Delcamp (Paris) - delcamp.net
          (cello) Fernand Pollain - Durand 9546, Paris, 1918

Ornaments are played variously as grace notes (acciaccaturas and appogiaturas),
mordents and trills in different editions of the sheet music.
Some editions prefer high frets; other editions prefer low frets / open strings.

The MIDI tempo is ~33 bpm (half notes). There is no repeat.

measure 1 - in some editions the chord on the 1st fret is played with a barre
            on the 1st fret, and the next 4 notes are played on the 1st string

measure 13 - in some sheet editions all the notes are played on the high frets

measure 25 - most guitar arrangements have either E-G-D-F, E-B-D-F or E-G-B-D-F
             for the 1st chord, which all sound discordant (because of the E) --
             Bach transcribed this piece from his Cello Suite BWV 1011 -- the
             chord in the Pollain edition of BWV 1011 is G-B-F (transposed)
             which is not discordant, and this has been used in the MIDI -
